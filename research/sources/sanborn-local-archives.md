### Campus coverage — **the campus site is NOT on any online Sanborn sheet** (verified visually
via the 1919 key map: coverage ends ~0.5 mi west of Verdugo Rd at the city "Boundary Line").
The campus (built 1937) could only appear in the offline 1950 paste-up volumes.

- To view 1925/1950: ProQuest Digital Sanborn Maps — free remote access with an **LA Public
  Library card** (lapl.org e-resources). License-restricted: reference only, NOT publishable
  on the site.

## TASK B — Local archives

### Glendale Library, Arts & Culture — History Room
- Research by appointment, no card needed. **glendalehistoryroom@glendaleca.gov · 818-548-2037**
- Online: Calisphere collections (California Revealed 5,563 items; Leslie C. Brand 225 items;
  Tropico/Glendale brochures) — https://calisphere.org/institution/73/collections/
- Flickr "Verdugo Views": https://www.flickr.com/photos/glendalepubliclibrary/
- Promising Calisphere hits: "Intersection of Crestmont Court & North Verdugo Road, 1932"
  (adjacent to campus), "Horses and riders on Verdugo Road, 1912", "Verdugo Swim Stadium and
  Glendale Community College", "Golden State freeway interchange construction, 1961".
  SR-2-specific construction photos sparse online — History Room is the better bet.

### GCC's own archives
- Holds yearbooks (*Log* 1928→, *La Reata* 1937→), student papers (*El Galeon* 1927→,
  *El Vaquero* 1936→), photos. Pilot digitization went to the Pasadena Digital History
  Collaboration (current visibility unconfirmed).
- OAC finding aid: https://oac.cdlib.org/findaid/ark:/13030/c8nv9qj9/
- **Contact: library@glendale.edu**, ext. 5586/5581/5577; request form:
  https://glendale.libwizard.com/f/GCC-Archives-Request-Form
- Note: glendale.edu recently redesigned + June 2026 security incident — old history pages 404.

## TASK C — Milestone timeline (verified)

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| 1927 | Founded as Glendale Junior College; classes at Glendale Union HS (Broadway & Verdugo) |
| 1929–37 | Harvard School plant; 1934–37 partly in 15 tent bungalows |
| 1936 | 25-acre Verdugo Rd site acquired (bond + PWA grant) |
| 1936–37 | Campus built ($725,932, PWA + district; WPA graded/landscaped; architect George M. Lindsey) |
| **1937** | **Occupied 1500 N Verdugo Rd campus** |
| 1944 | Renamed Glendale College |
| 1970/71 | Separate district; renamed Glendale Community College |
| 1955–58 | SR-2 route approved (Dec 1955); first segment opened 1958 (south of here) |
| 1962 | I-5 interchange opened |
| 1972–78 | Segment past campus largely built by late 1972 but unopened (used as film location); **opened late spring 1978** |
